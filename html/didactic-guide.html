Relationship between tasks and assessment criteria.

In this learning situation, each task is directly connected to specific assessment criteria in order to ensure a coherent and meaningful evaluation process.

The different project sessions are designed to progressively develop the students’ skills:

• Session 1–2: Activation of prior knowledge and vocabulary learning
→ Related to criterion ING.5.1 (understanding information)

• Session 3–4: Practice of vocabulary and communicative interaction (role-play, directions)
→ Related to criteria ING.5.2 (oral and written production) and ING.5.3 (interaction)

• Session 5–6: Research and information selection about London and sustainable transport
→ Related to criteria ING.5.1 (comprehension) and ING.5.5 (learning strategies)

• Session 7: Design of the sustainable itinerary
→ Related to criteria ING.5.2 (written production) and ING.5.6 (cultural awareness)

• Session 8: Oral presentation of the project
→ Related to criteria ING.5.2 (oral production), ING.5.3 (interaction) and ING.5.6 (intercultural awareness)

• Session 9: Final reflection and self-assessment
→ Related to criteria ING.5.5 (reflection) and ING.5.6 (cultural awareness)

This structure ensures that all assessment criteria are addressed through meaningful tasks, allowing students to demonstrate their learning in different ways.

Additionally, this approach supports a continuous and formative assessment process, where both the learning process and the final product are taken into account.
